OneClass.


Background

When I joined OneClass, the company was experiencing rapid growth across multiple education business units. Backed by one of Taiwan’s leading educational publishers, Nani Book Enterprise, the platform served schools, tutoring centres, teachers, parents, and students at scale.


However, internal operations had evolved organically. Each department relied on its own workflows, spreadsheets, and legacy systems, resulting in fragmented processes, duplicated data, and increasing operational complexity.


As Product Manager, I led the redesign of the company's internal operations platform, transforming disconnected business processes into a unified system that supported organisation-wide collaboration and scalability.


The Challenge

The operational platform supported eight business functions:


  • Sales
  • Marketing
  • Customer Support
  • Scheduling
  • Student Advisory
  • Teacher Management
  • Teacher Training
  • Curriculum Management

Each team had different processes, tools, and data structures. Many critical workflows were still managed manually or through offline Excel files, creating inefficiencies and making cross-functional collaboration difficult.


The goal was not simply to build new features, but to create a scalable internal platform that unified operational workflows, data, and permissions across the organisation.


My Role

I owned the product lifecycle from discovery to delivery, including:


  • Conducting business process analysis across eight operational teams
  • Defining product strategy and platform roadmap
  • Designing operational workflows and system architecture
  • Aligning stakeholders across operations, engineering, and leadership
  • Writing PRDs, user stories, and acceptance criteria
  • Leading Agile delivery through sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives

By working closely with engineering and design teams, I translated complex operational requirements into scalable internal systems.


Workflow Transformation

A core responsibility of my role was analysing and redesigning business processes across multiple departments.



Through stakeholder interviews and workflow mapping, I identified manual bottlenecks and disconnected responsibilities, then collaborated with engineering to transform these into automated platform workflows.


The result was an operational platform that improved collaboration between departments while reducing repetitive manual work and increasing process consistency across the organisation.


Data Architecture & Platform Scalability

Beyond product planning, I worked closely with engineering leadership on platform architecture and technical strategy.


Legacy user information was fragmented across offline Excel files and multiple databases, making maintenance and reporting increasingly difficult.


To improve scalability, we:


  • Refactored user profile data into unified data models
  • Migrated fragmented data sources into a centralised system
  • Improved data consistency across operational teams
  • Established scalable foundations for future platform expansion

This work strengthened the reliability of internal operations while enabling more effective reporting and cross-functional collaboration.


Product Discovery & Validation

Alongside platform development, I conducted market research and user interviews to identify opportunities for improving the learning experience.



Working with students, parents, and customer support teams, I developed and tested a gamified Learning Point MVP designed to increase classroom engagement and course participation.

Through moderated usability testing and A/B experiments, 75% of participating students reported greater willingness to attend classes after interacting with the improved experience.

Following successful validation, I designed the full Learning Point product, including workflows, reward mechanisms, and implementation wireframes.


Data-Driven Product Management

To measure platform adoption, I integrated Google Analytics into the internal system and analysed feature usage across operational departments.

Combined with user surveys conducted across eight business units, these insights were translated into a prioritised product backlog that guided roadmap planning and continuous platform optimisation.


Agile Delivery

I led cross-functional product delivery using Agile methodologies, facilitating daily stand-ups, sprint planning, kickoff meetings, sprint reviews, and retrospectives.

By maintaining close collaboration between engineering, design, QA, and operational stakeholders, the team consistently delivered high-quality platform improvements while remaining aligned on business priorities.


Strategic Product Planning

After relocating to the UK in 2023, I continued leading discovery and planning for the next generation of operational systems, including teacher recruitment, student matching, and payroll management.

The final proposal included:


  • Business process analysis
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Competitor research
  • Workflow redesign
  • Product roadmap
  • Wireframes
  • Development scope

The proposal received stakeholder alignment and became the final platform initiative I led at OneClass.


Key Contributions

  • Led the redesign of OneClass's internal operations platform serving eight business functions
  • Unified fragmented workflows across CRM, scheduling, teacher operations, curriculum management, and payment systems
  • Conducted business process analysis and translated operational requirements into scalable system design
  • Collaborated with engineering to refactor legacy data structures and improve platform scalability
  • Built operational dashboards that enabled data-driven decision-making across departments
  • Managed end-to-end product delivery through stakeholder alignment and Agile execution
  • Validated and designed a gamified Learning Point product through user research and A/B testing
