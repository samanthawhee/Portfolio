Improving  Advanced  Learning  &  AI  Value  in  Duolingo .

Overview

Duolingo’s growth from approximately $161M in revenue in 2020 to $748M in 2024 highlights the effectiveness of its gamified approach to language learning. The product is designed to build daily habits through short, engaging lessons, while expanding into AI-driven features and premium subscription tiers. With over 100 million monthly active users and strong growth in paid subscriptions, Duolingo has established itself as a leading platform in the digital language learning market.


Problem

Duolingo is highly effective at engaging beginner learners through gamified tasks and reward-based mechanisms that encourage daily usage. However, feedback from paid users, particularly those using AI-powered features, indicates growing dissatisfaction. Common issues include repetitive responses, inconsistent explanation quality, and limited depth in learning.


As a result, users experience a plateau in learning progress, especially at intermediate and advanced levels, where more contextual and practical language use is required.


Why This Matters

This challenge reflects a gap between technical capability and business objectives. As Duolingo continues to expand its user base, it naturally attracts learners at different proficiency levels, each with distinct needs. While gamification successfully drives engagement for beginners, it does not fully support users seeking deeper learning outcomes.


At the same time, AI is becoming central to the language learning industry. Over half of digital learning platforms already integrate AI-driven personalisation, which has been shown to significantly improve retention. Learners increasingly expect tailored learning paths, contextual feedback, and conversational practice that feels natural and responsive.


If Duolingo does not strengthen the value of its AI-powered features, particularly within its premium tiers, it risks losing advanced users to alternative tools that provide more flexible and context-aware learning experiences.


User Segments

Duolingo serves a broad audience, but three key segments highlight this gap. Beginner learners benefit most from gamified, bite-sized lessons that build consistency and confidence. Paid users expect a smoother experience and more effective learning outcomes in return for their subscription. Advanced learners, however, remain underserved, as they require more realistic conversational practice and deeper, more personalised feedback to continue progressing.


Root Cause

The issue stems from a mismatch between learning design and user progression. Duolingo’s current structure prioritises scalability and engagement, which works well for early-stage learning. However, as users progress, their needs shift toward contextual understanding, localisation, and real-world communication.


AI features are currently positioned as add-ons within premium tiers rather than being fully integrated into the core learning experience. This limits their impact and creates a perception that the value does not justify the cost. As a result, users may turn to alternative AI tools that offer more flexible and interactive learning experiences.


Opportunity

Industry trends indicate that AI is no longer a supporting feature but a core component of modern learning platforms. Advances in conversational AI enable systems to recognise tone, context, and user intent, allowing for more natural and adaptive interactions. Emerging technologies such as pronunciation visualisation and immersive simulations further enhance learning by providing real-time, actionable feedback.


In addition, there is growing demand for content that reflects real-world usage, including regional variations, cultural context, and informal language. These trends present an opportunity to reposition AI at the centre of the learning experience, rather than as a supplementary feature.


Solution

To improve the experience for advanced learners, Duolingo should shift toward a more AI-driven and context-based learning model. Instead of relying primarily on structured exercises, the platform can introduce scenario-based interactions where users engage in realistic conversations with AI.


These interactions can be guided by structured prompts to support learning while still allowing for spontaneous dialogue. Expanding the range of conversation topics would better reflect real-world situations, enabling users to practice language in meaningful contexts. At the same time, AI-generated feedback should become more consistent and personalised, helping users understand not only what is incorrect, but why.


To further enhance speaking skills, visual pronunciation support, such as animated or AR-based guidance, could be introduced to help users improve articulation. Increasing variability and personalisation within conversations would also make interactions feel more natural and less repetitive, improving both engagement and learning effectiveness.


Prioritisation

In the short term, improving the consistency and quality of AI-generated feedback would deliver immediate value with relatively low implementation effort. In the medium term, expanding conversational practice across more languages would strengthen engagement and better support user progression. In the long term, developing more immersive and context-rich learning experiences would differentiate the product and support scalable growth.


Metrics

As these improvements primarily target premium users, success should be measured through performance within higher-tier subscriptions. Key metrics include retention among advanced users, increased engagement with AI features, and improved conversion rates to premium tiers. Additionally, tracking user progression beyond intermediate levels would provide a clearer indication of learning effectiveness.


Summary

By strengthening AI-driven learning within its premium offerings, Duolingo can significantly improve the experience for advanced users. Shifting toward a more contextual and conversational approach would enable more authentic language practice while reinforcing the value of its AI features. In the long term, positioning AI at the centre of the learning experience will be key to sustaining both user engagement and business growth.
